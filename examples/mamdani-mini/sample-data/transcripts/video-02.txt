Okay. I voted yes on the Riverside Park closure. I know some of you are mad about that and I want to tell you why I did it.

The drainage system at Riverside is older than I am. City Engineer Robert Huang told us in committee that his team has been patching it for three years and the patches are starting to fail faster than they can put them in. If we wait another year, we are not looking at a planned four-month closure. We are looking at an emergency closure, probably in the middle of summer, that costs two to three times as much.

The 2.3 million dollar price tag covers the drainage replacement, new playground equipment to replace the 2004 stuff that's already been flagged twice by safety inspectors, resurfacing the walking trails, and lighting upgrades that District 4 parents have been asking me for since I took office.

Yes, four months is a long time. The closure starts April 1 and is supposed to wrap by August 1. I pushed for the city to relocate youth sports to Memorial Field for the season, and that's in the plan. The Riverside Community Center stays open the whole time.

Is this perfect? No. Is closing a park in the spring a tough call? Absolutely. But the alternative — and I want to be really clear about this — the alternative is a worse closure later. I'm not going to leave that for the next councilmember to handle. Talk to me at the next town hall. I'll be there.

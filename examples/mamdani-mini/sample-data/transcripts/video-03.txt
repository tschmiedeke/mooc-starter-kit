A lot of you have been emailing me about the contractor on the Riverside Park job. I hear you and I want to address it directly because I don't think silence is the right move here.

The contract went to Meridian Infrastructure LLC. They were selected through the city's competitive bidding process. Three firms bid. Meridian was the lowest qualified bidder by about 180 thousand dollars on a 2.3 million dollar job. The selection happened in committee in February and the council confirmed it in March.

I've seen the messages asking me about Meridian's track record on the East Side library project in 2023. Yes, that project ran six weeks over schedule. I asked the same question. The Public Works director gave me the after-action report and I read it. The delay there was a supply chain issue with steel framing, not a Meridian crew problem, and the city's contract terms have been updated since to push that risk back onto the contractor.

Am I saying everything is going to go perfectly? No. What I am saying is that the process worked the way it's supposed to work, and if it goes sideways I will be the first one asking hard questions. I want you to hold me to that.

If you have something specific — a document, a name, anything — send it to my office. Don't post it and assume I'll see it. Send it. My email is in the bio.

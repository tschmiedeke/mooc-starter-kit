Hey District 4, it's Yvonne. I want to spend a few minutes walking you through what I'm fighting for in this year's budget, because I think it matters and I don't want you to hear about it for the first time when the vote happens.

The headline number is 4.8 million dollars. That's what I'm asking the council to put toward parks and recreation in District 4 over the next fiscal year. I know that sounds like a lot, and it is, but here's how it breaks down. About 2.3 million goes to the Riverside Park rebuild we already approved. The rest — roughly 2.5 million — is for three things. Number one, a new playground at Lincoln Park, because the equipment there is from 2008 and it shows. Number two, expanded summer programming for kids ages 5 to 12, because right now we turn families away every June. And number three, a part-time forestry position so we stop losing trees we could have saved.

Where's the money coming from? I'm proposing we pull it from the unrestricted reserves, which the city manager confirmed last week are sitting at about 17 million. That leaves us well above the 10 percent reserve floor the council adopted in 2022.

I know parks aren't the only thing on the budget. Public safety is on there. Roads are on there. But parks pay back. Every dollar we put into a kid's summer is a dollar we don't spend later. Talk to me. Email me. I read everything.

# Viral claim fact-check exercise

## The claim

> "Drinking coffee first thing in the morning spikes your cortisol levels and causes anxiety. You should wait 90-120 minutes after waking before having caffeine."

**Source:** Popular wellness influencer post (2.3M views)

---

## Your task

Use Claude to help research and fact-check this claim. Work through these steps:

### Step 1: Break down the claim

What are the specific, testable assertions?

1. Morning coffee raises cortisol levels
2. This elevated cortisol causes anxiety
3. Waiting 90-120 minutes prevents this effect

### Step 2: Research prompts to try

Ask Claude questions like:

- "What does peer-reviewed research say about caffeine and cortisol?"
- "Is there evidence that timing caffeine intake affects cortisol response?"
- "What's the normal cortisol cycle after waking?"

### Step 3: Evaluate sources

For each study or claim Claude mentions, ask:

- "Can you provide the citation for that study?"
- "How large was the sample size?"
- "Has this been replicated?"

### Step 4: Write your verdict

Based on your research, rate the claim:

- [ ] True
- [ ] Mostly true
- [ ] Mixed evidence
- [ ] Mostly false
- [ ] False

---

## Notes

This claim is good for practice because:

- It contains multiple sub-claims
- Some parts have research support, others don't
- The "90-120 minutes" number is specific and checkable
- It's widely shared but often without citations

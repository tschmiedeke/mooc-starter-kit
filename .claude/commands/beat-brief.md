# Beat brief

Read every file in `sample-docs/` in this repo. Treat them as the day's incoming material on the Greenfield city council beat: meeting minutes, press releases, interview notes, tip emails, and a viral claim that needs checking.

Then draft a short daily briefing for the reporter covering this beat. The briefing should have three parts:

## 1. Five bullets

Five bullets, no more. Each bullet is one sentence. Lead with the most newsworthy item. Each bullet must include:

- What happened or what was said
- Who said it (with title if relevant)
- The source file the bullet is drawn from, in parentheses

Use sentence case. No Title Case. No exclamation points. Plain news voice.

## 2. Attribution flags

List any claim from the source documents that is presented as fact but is not attributed to a named source or a primary document. For each one, name the file it came from and quote the unattributed claim verbatim. If everything is properly attributed, say so explicitly.

## 3. What to follow up

A short list of follow-up actions for the reporter. Each item should be something the reporter could do in under 30 minutes: a phone call to make, a record to pull, a number to verify, a person to find. Be specific. "Call the mayor's office to confirm the $2 million figure in the press release" beats "verify the budget claim."

End the briefing there. Do not add a closing paragraph or summary.
